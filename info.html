What is Information Asymmetry?

According to Merriam-Webster dictionary, asymmetric information is the information relating to a transaction in which one party has relevant information that is not known by or available to the other party.

Asymmetric information contributes to invisible control and uneven power distribution in our society.

Investopia.com specifies two main kinds of information asymmetry.

Adverse Selection

  • One party has more information about the areas of interest than the other party leading to material damage to the other party.
  • In case of insurance, buyers typically have more knowledge about their health than the insurance providers, so insurance companies reduce exposure to large claims by raising premiums or putting limits on coverage.
  • Generally, sellers have more information about the products or services than buyers.

Moral Hazard

  • When a party to a contract has no negative consequence of taking the risks, moral hazard can occur.
  • Moral hazard can occur in various settings, including employer-employee relationships, lending and insurance businesses.
  • For example, an investment bank that takes risky practices typically make huge profits during the economic upturn, but when any financial crisis occurs they get a large chunk of government bailout while their customers file for bankruptcy.
  • Undetected moral hazard can be more prevalent when information is concentrated in one or few areas.
